Deep comparative evaluation

The Diamond 9 is the gold standard for prioritizing complex ideas. By forcing a single definitive top and bottom choice while clustering the nuanced middle, it demands careful justification and sparks high-quality collaborative debate.

How to Use ThinkRank Studio

Setting Up Your Activity

Choose a layout from the dropdown matching your goals. You can select from 13 different subjects and 130 pre-built topics to auto-fill questions, or type your entirely custom "Idea" text boxes.

  • Random Example: Instantly fills the board with a random educational or professional premise.
  • Timer: Optional countdown timer. Setting "Auto-start" triggers it immediately when participants load the page.
  • Allow Edit: When toggled off, participants viewing your link cannot click "Back to Edit" to tamper with the text boxes. Perfect for tests!

Sharing Your Configuration

You don't need accounts or saving buttons to share your custom activities.

When you hit "Generate", the URL perfectly updates. You can literally just Copy & Paste your browser's link to anyone—their screen will load exactly what you made!

Alternatively, use the Share Link / QR button on the edit screen to throw a massive QR code on the projector or quickly grab a clean shortlink for your team.

Best Practices & Use Cases

Running a Team Meeting

ThinkRank is a secret weapon for breaking meeting deadlocks and forcing alignment.

  • The "Elimination" Tie-Breaker: Throw conflicting ideas into the Elimination Bracket. It forces a binary choice (A vs B) instead of vague compromises.
  • Feature Prioritization: Enter 9 product features into the Diamond 9 layout. Have the team collaboratively agree on what makes the "Top 1" and the "Bottom 1" before sorting the middle.

Starter Activity for a Lesson

Perfect as a "Do Now" hook as students walk into the classroom.

  • Line Ranking (Top 5): Ask students to organize 5 historical figures from most to least influential. Give them exactly 3 minutes on the timer.
  • QR Code Handoff: Put the generated QR code on your smartboard. Students scan it with their iPads, dragging the boxes to prove their understanding before you review it together.
